Our Story

A name with weight. A mission with purpose.

In 1921, the Greenwood district of Tulsa, Oklahoma was the wealthiest Black community in America — a thriving district of Black-owned banks, businesses, and homes that the nation came to call Black Wall Street. In two days, a white mob destroyed it. The wealth was extracted; the loss was never repaired.

Generations later, the Racial Equity Institute gave that history a framework. Its groundwater analysis taught a movement to stop asking why individuals fail and start examining the systems — redlining, lending, ownership — that produce the same racial outcomes by design. Black Wall Street LLC is that analysis turned into action.

Today, we are a community-focused real estate and development firm headquartered in Greensboro, North Carolina. We rebuild the neighborhoods systemic policy drained, expand homeownership where it was denied, and create commercial spaces where local families and businesses can build wealth and stay.

We confront systemic inequity directly — not with slogans, but with deeds, keys, and closing tables.

We don't just build properties. We rebuild possibility.

— Black Wall Street LLC
